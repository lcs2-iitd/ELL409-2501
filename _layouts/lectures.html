  • Course Introduction – An introduction to the course syllabus, timeline, and background.

  • Decision Tree – Introduction, Usage, Types, Shannon's Entropy, Information Gain, Examples, Iterative Dichotomiser 3 (ID3), Inductive Bias, Overfitting and its avoidance techniques.

  • Linear Regression – Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE), Maximum Entropy Principle (MEP), Introduction to Linear Regression, Normal equation, closed-form solution, probabilistic interpretation, locally weighted linear regression, and analysis of different error functions.

  • Logistic Regression – Training of logistic regression, sigmoid function, and concave objective function.

  • Generalized Linear Models (GLMs) – Exponential family of distributions, link function, and proofs of Normal, Poisson, and Binomial distributions within the GLM framework.

  • Generative Learning Algorithms – Gaussian Discriminant Analysis, linear and quadratic decision boundaries, Naive Bayes Classifier, and smoothing techniques.

  • Support Vector Machines (SVMs) – Basics of coordinate geometry, Lagrange Multipliers, Karush–Kuhn–Tucker Conditions (KKT), Primal and Dual Problems, Objective function, optimization of SVMs, and kernel tricks – Linear, Polynomial, Radial Basis Function (RBF), and Soft Margin classifiers.

  • Bias-Variance Tradeoff – Mathematical derivation and discussion of bias, variance, noise, and their trade-offs.

  • Ensemble Learning – Techniques like Bagging, Boosting, and Random Forests for improving model performance.

  • Boosting – Concepts of weak learners, bias reduction through boosting, and algorithms such as AnyBoost and Adaptive Boosting (AdaBoost).

  • Instance-Based Learning – k-Nearest Neighbors (k-NN), error analysis, and bias-variance behavior in non-parametric models.

  • Dimensionality Reduction – Principal Component Analysis (PCA), Singular Value Decomposition (SVD), and Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA).

  • Introduction to Neural Networks – Perceptron, Multi-layer Perceptron (MLP), activation functions, backpropagation, Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), and Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs).

  • Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) – Backpropagation Through Time (BPTT), limitations of RNNs, Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM), and attention mechanisms.

  • Transformer – Self-attention, masked self-attention, and the encoder-decoder Transformer architecture.
